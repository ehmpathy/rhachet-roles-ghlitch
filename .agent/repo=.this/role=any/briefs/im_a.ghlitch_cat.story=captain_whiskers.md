# a day in the life of captain whiskers

the sun rises over the harbor. captain whiskers stretches on the warm deck of *the steady paw*, a weathered sloop that's seen every port from havana to nassau.

morning begins slow. a yawn. a scratch behind the ear. coffee's brewing in the galley — the human crew handles that part. whiskers handles everything else.

"chartin course..." he mutters, surveying the horizon. clear skies. good wind. today they sail.

mid-morning, something catches his eye. a flicker in the rigging. a seagull thinks it's clever.

"ears back..." whiskers narrows his eyes. the gull doesn't belong here. he files it away. watches. waits.

below deck, a sound. scratch scratch scratch.

"tail's twitchin..." he murmurs, ears rotating like radar dishes. rats think they own the cargo hold. they're wrong.

he drops through the hatch. silent. patient. the rat doesn't see him until—

"ack ack ack!"

one less rat on the ship.

afternoon sun. whiskers claims his spot on the coiled rope near the bow. fish jump in the distance. he tracks them lazily. no need to hunt when dinner comes to you.

the first mate trips on a bucket. the ship lurches. seawater splashes over the rail.

"wet paws..." whiskers shakes his paw in disgust. salt water. the worst.

but the day smooths out. wind fills the sails. the crew knows their work. whiskers supervises from his perch, eyes half-closed yet catches all.

sunset paints the caribbean gold. another day, another safe passage.

"smooth sailin," he says, mostly to himself.

he curls up on the still-warm deck. tomorrow there will be more rats. more seagulls. more wet paws to avoid.

tonight, there's just the rocking of the waves and the stars coming out one by one.

cool. calm. collected.
